Investment Process

The application procedure, which is completed by registration through the website or email, is when we first come into contact with the entrepreneurs and their unique tech-infused vision.

The choice to be observed or rejected is determined following a review of the idea's criteria, viability, and capacity to generate a return on investment in several tiers of the screening process.

By assessing and casting votes, an investment committee is formed. In light of the screening evaluation, the elected committee members decide whether to move forward with the idea.

A complete due diligence audit report is produced after the committee's conclusion. Thus, it makes it possible to specify the idea's legality, intricacy, and mechanicality.

In the end, the investment is approved after all the technicalities and analyses have been conducted to validate the idea's viability and selected criteria.

Initial Public Offerings (IPOs), Strategic Acquisitions, and Management Buyouts are the three exit strategies that are often used.
